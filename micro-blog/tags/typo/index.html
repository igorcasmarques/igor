Divorced Dads

May 24, 2024
Igor Coelho A. S. Marques
Technical writer in Ottawa, Canada

Comedian Graeme Barrett has a hilarious series on YouTube called Divorced Dads. It's about a fictional collectible card game à la Magic: The Gathering. When Barrett released the card game for real, I immediately ordered some packs.

The rules are simple and fit on a double-sided 8 x 11 cm (not in) card. Part of the charm of Divorced Dads is its low production value, so I was not surprised to find a glaring typo on the rule card: "scarified" (intentionally scarred for aesthetic reasons) rather than "sacrificed" (discarded from the playing field).

Will collectors one day be willing to pay thousands of dollars for this first-edition misspelled rule card?

Probably not.

Typo 7

April 15, 2024
Igor Coelho A. S. Marques
Technical writer in Ottawa, Canada

The tween in me thinks this misspelling is funny because of another word that ends with those two syllables, but I also don't judge the people who made the typo. Schwa (ə) is the most common vowel sound in English because any vowel can make it in an unstressed syllable. Change the "a" in "receptacle" for any other vowel and the word still sounds the same.

It's also interesting that the sign tells us to think of the employees who have to empty the bin. I assume this is to prevent people from disposing of messy garbage because the bin isn't lined with a bag, but I'm not sure. Whatever the reason, it must have been a recurring issue that made them feel the need to include this plea on the sign.

Jockvale School 1

February 29, 2024
Igor Coelho A. S. Marques
Technical writer in Ottawa, Canada

I went back to Bruce S. Elliott's The City Beyond to see if his comments about Nepean's School Section 10 (the historical Jockvale School House) would help me discover the origin of the Strandherd name. No such luck but I learned that several historical maps depict SS 10, which I've included in the GIF below.

SS 10 was founded by Irish Catholics in 1841, not long after they'd settled in the area, so I assume that the school's location was already geographically significant and accessible by then. This means that Strandherd has been there between Jockvale and Greenbank (and likely between Borrisokane and Longfields) at least since the 1840s. It's an important part of Barrhaven's history.

As for the Strandherd name, it was probably given to the road in the mid 20th century by some developer or city planner, and it's unlikely it has a historical tie to 19th-century Nepean. The earliest mention of the name I could find in the Ottawa Citizen is from 1974.

End of the road. I'm starting to feel like Max in Aronofsky's Pi (1998). What started out as OC Transpo's glaring typo turned out to be ... just a typo, after all. It's time to shower, go outside for some -10° fresh air, and move on.
